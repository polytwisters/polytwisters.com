Polytwisters

Polytwisters are a family of strange four-dimensional shapes related to polyhedra, but with cylindrically curved instead of flat sides. They were discovered by mathematician Jonathan Bowers in 2007, and are deeply related to a concept known as Hopf fibration.

This site is a noncommercial project dedicated to exploration of these curious objects. Currently there's not much here, but you can read an explanation of polytwisters.
